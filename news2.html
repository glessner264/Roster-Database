Bulldogs helping out

April 5th, 2013

Published By: Joseph Cress, The Sentinel

Packing furniture for moving Friday hit close to home for Web Hersperger of Carlisle.


The local Rotarian knows what it must be like for Hurricane Sandy victims living in the area of Toms River, N.J.


His son-in-law Michael Hennessy is helping to rebuild a family home destroyed at Breezy Point, Long Island, during the late October superstorm.


Hersperger was among dozens of volunteers who helped the Carlisle Rotary Club load up trucks Friday afternoon with donated furniture and books. The trucks depart this morning from an Allen Distribution warehouse on Alexander Spring Road, South Middleton Township, where the furniture had been in storage since late November.


Rotarians from Carlisle and Mechanicsburg were joined by members of the Big Spring High School football team, the Carlisle High School girls’ soccer team and other high school students.


“To see the community working together is such a positive thing,” Hersperger said. “It points out the strength of Carlisle. I am very proud of the Rotary and its involvement not just locally but all over the world.”
